Wicked: Part 1 (2024)

Directed by: Jon M. Chu
Set prior to the events of Wizard of Oz, Wicked: Part 1 follows Elphaba (Cynthia Erivo), a young woman born with green skin, who is eventually accepted into the prestigious Shiz Academy to practice and develop her magic abilities. A rivalry forms with Galinda (Ariana Grande), a fellow student who aims to ascend to the height of popularity and prestige. As Elphaba engages with her studies and competes with Galinda, a friendship blossoms between the two. However, a sinister plot brewing in the darker corners of Shiz may just threaten not only Elphaba and Galinda's relationship, but also the broader world of Oz.

Godzilla Minus One (2023)

Directed by: Takashi Yamazaki
After an encounter with the ferocious Godzilla, Koichi (Ryunosuke Kamiki), a kamikaze pilot who deserted his unit, grapples with PTSD brought on by the encounter while trying to start a new life for himself. A chance at redemption comes when Godzilla returns to terrorize the seas and cities of Japan.

The Banshees of Inisherin (2022)

Directed by: Martin McDonagh
In a sleepy town in coastal Ireland, a friendship between two men, Padraic (Colin Farrell) and Colm (Brendan Gleeson) soon comes to an abrupt end, when one day Colm approaches Padraic, claiming he does not want to be friends any longer. The film follows Padraic as he tries to repair the friendship and together with his sister, Siobhan (Kerry Condon), works to uncover what motivated Colm's decision. Padraic's timeframe soon grows shorter after Colm delivers a terrifying ultimatum.

Whiplash (2014)

Directed by: Damien Chazelle
With a dream of becoming one of the greatest drummers in the world, Andrew Neiman (Miles Teller) soon joins Schafer Music Studio, run by prolific teacher Terrence Fletcher (J.K. Simmons), who's strict, highly confrontational teaching style pushes Neiman to his physical and psychological limits. As Neiman performs and strives to improve his drumming abilities, under Fletcher's ruthless tutelage, the line between passion and obsession become progressively blurred.

Kung-Fu Panda (2008)

Directed by: John Stevenson and Mark Osborne
Po (Jack Black), a panda working at a noodle shop in ancient China, soon has his dreams to become a master of kung-fu realized when he is chosen as the Dragon Warrior-- a martial arts master destined to fight evil. Along with his heroes, the Furious Five, he trains to master kung fu in time to combat the villainous Tai-Lung (Ian McShane), a former disciple of his master Shifu (Dustin Hoffman).

Spider-Man (2002)

Directed by: Sam Raimi
Adapted from the comic book series of the same name, this film follows Peter Parker (Tobey Maguire), a teenager who after being bitten by a radioactive spider gains super-strength and web-slinging abilities. As the superhero Spider-Man he learns of the responsibilities of being a hero while stopping crime and saving the city from the evil Green Goblin (Willem Dafoe).

Directed by: Rob Reiner
An encounter between two college students, Harry (Billy Crystal) and Sally (Meg Ryan), leaves the pair curious if men and women can ever strictly remain friends. Ten years later, they meet up again, with their respective best friends, and reopen the argument as they start to spend more time with each other.

Die Hard (1988)

Directed by: John McTiernan
A action classic of the late 80s, Die Hard follows police detective John McClane (Bruce Willis) as he's forced to contend with Hans Gruber (Alan Rickman) and his squad of terrorists who have seized control of a Los Angeles skyscraper and taken its residents--one of which being McClane's ex-wife--hostage, with threat of death.

Halloween (1978)

Directed by: John Carpenter
15 years after his institutionalization, serial killer Michael Myers reemerges in the town of Haddonfield, Illinois stalking and deadset on taking the life of teenage babysitter Laurie Strode (Jamie Lee Curtis) and her circle of friends. On Myers' trail is his former psychiatrist, Sam Loomis (Donald Pleasance), who seeks to prevent Myers' killing spree and capture him once and for all.

Bridge on the River Kwai (1957)

Directed by: David Lean
A David Lean epic taking place in WW2 about British POWs led by Colonel Nicholson (Alec Guiness) tasked by a Japanese commandante (Sessue Hayakawa) with constructing a bridge over the titular river. The film explores the tensions within the prison camp as the population of POWs is split between those willing to comply and others choosing to rebel against their captors.
